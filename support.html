Support

Answers to common questions — and a way to reach us if you need more.

Frequently Asked Questions

SurfsUp fetches live data from the internet. Make sure your device has an active connection. If you've reached your daily API credit limit (200 for free / 1,000 for SurfsUp+), conditions won't refresh until the following day. Pull down on the beach list to manually refresh.

The Surf Score is a 0–100 rating calculated from five factors: wave height, wind speed, weather conditions, sea surface temperature, and air temperature. Higher scores mean better surfing conditions. A score above 80 is considered epic.

BeachBuddy is an AI assistant powered by the Llama language model via Groq. It knows the current conditions for your selected beach and can answer questions about surfing, safety, nearby places, and more. Free users get 5 conversations per day; SurfsUp+ users get 100.

Go to the Search tab, search for a beach by name, then tap the ★ star icon on the result. The beach will be saved to your Home screen. Your favourites are stored on your device and sync to Apple Watch automatically.

Free users get real-time conditions, surf scores, beach search up to 50 km, 200 API credits/day, and 5 BeachBuddy conversations/day. SurfsUp+ unlocks the 7-Day Forecast, 100 BeachBuddy conversations/day, 1,000 API credits/day, and beach search up to 350 km.

Subscriptions are managed entirely by Apple. Go to iPhone Settings → your name → Subscriptions, find SurfsUp+, and tap Cancel Subscription. You'll retain access until the end of your current billing period.

Open SurfsUp and go to Settings. Tap "Restore Purchases" at the bottom of the subscription section. Make sure you're signed in with the same Apple ID you used to purchase SurfsUp+.

Location is used to find beaches near you and to determine whether it's daytime or nighttime for the Adaptive theme. SurfsUp never stores your location on any server. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

The watch app receives data from your iPhone via Bluetooth. Open SurfsUp on your iPhone and bring it to the foreground — it will send your latest favourites and conditions to the watch within a few seconds. A SurfsUp+ subscription is required to use the Apple Watch app.

Go to the Settings tab and tap the Units row under Preferences. You can switch between Imperial (°F / ft) and Metric (°C / m) at any time.
